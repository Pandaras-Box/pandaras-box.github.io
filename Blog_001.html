I didn't catch that…what exactly IS Tranzfuser? (& some E3 thoughts)

So we've alluded to being involved with Tranzfuser recently, and I've been hoping to get our gamedev blog up and running finally in a regular capacity, so I suppose explaining Tranzfuser is as good a way to start as any! We'll wrap up this post with a few thoughts on all the E3 goings-on too. (The plan is these posts will land each week around the Tuesday/Wednesday mark going forward.)

Tranzfuser is a games development graduate competition that has been set up and funded by the lovely folks at @UKGamesTalent (the same group of folks behind the @UKGamesFund if you've heard of that) that we applied for and successfully got placed in last month. We pitched a new concept (more on that next week) and it went down well, so with a little bit of funding in our pot now, we've been tasked with prototyping the concept within 10 weeks, beginning last Monday (6/6/16). Throughout the scheme, we will be supported by mentors, all experienced in the industry, locally through @NIScreen. In September we'll be hopping over to England to demo the results of our hard work publicly, and, if it's received well? There's a fantastic prize… namely, we get further funding to develop the prototype into something we can bring to market! Naturally the team is very excited to be back in the saddle with our own IP, and we're looking forward to revealing the concept and what we've worked on already in the coming weeks.

Before that though, we'll wrap up the blog this week with what's caught our attention this week in the industry - it being E3 week, there's a lot more than I'd expect in later blogs. You may have caught my livetweeting of the reveals on our Twitter feed during the conferences but I thought that, given a few hours to process the last couple of nights of announcements, we'd take a run through the titles that have caught the eye of the team (at least those of us who caught the news so far).

First, a quick shoutout to @RareLtd with SeaOfThieves, looks very cool and our own Keith (@IM_KStocky) has been over there working on it for his placement year - well done, Keith!

David so far has been fairly reserved with what has caught his eye across the show, cautiously optimistic with Ben and myself about WatchDogs2, the lighter tone and greater freedom in application of "hacks" being notable. He also noted interest in HaloWars2, DeadRising4, Ghost Recon Wildlands (more on that below) and Dishonored2. Steep from Ubisoft is his personal highlight thus far, due to it having been a while since a decent looking snowboarding game was released, the genre being fairly unpopulated and, well, the snow effects being "purdy"! His take on it is that it looks like a fun cross between the freedom of movement of SSX and Just Cause 3 (and, of course, the snowsports angle).

Similarly reserved, Ben was nonetheless happy to see the return of CrashBandicoot as more than just simply a Skylanders-related headline-grabber, with a remaster of the classic @NaughtyDog developed trilogy being announced. The SpiderManPS4 reveal, along with @HIDEO_KOJIMA_EN's return with Dead Stranding (and @wwwbigbaldhead to boot!), were notable for him (as well as Dicky and myself), and Kojima's entrance during Sony's conference felt like a wonderful #F**kKonami after the horrible stuff that came out of that publisher over the last year or so. Ben is also intrigued to see what comes of FalloutVR, gave a suitable "frak yea!" to South Park: The Fractured But Whole and was happy to see more Titanfall, particularly now that this sequel has single-player and "isn't a DLC fest like Battlefield 1".

Much more broadly excited was Dicky, despite his thinking he hadn't been interested in all that wide a variety. Along with the other guys, Dicky liked the look of Steep, Dishonored 2, GR: Wildlands, South Park and Dead Rising 4. Further zombie-killing caught his eye with State of Decay 2 and DaysGone ("Sons of Anarchy meets Walking Dead" by my reckoning, although it's hard to judge at this stage if it's free-roaming or scripted set-pieces). @Obsidian delighted him with Tyranny and the suggestion of huge variety of choice in Detroit: Become Human impressed. Also of note for him were Aragami, Warhammer 40K: Dawn of War 3 and WeHappyFew (gotta agree with him on this particularly).

With Ciaran away, that just leaves myself, so I'll start off with Ghost ReconWildlands (see? I said I'd return to that). After an impressive debut last year at E3, the game caught the attention of the entire team this year. Suggesting a similar freedom to tackle missions similar to one of Ubisoft's more recent Tom Clancy titles (Splinter Cell: Blacklist), the game presents a vast landscape with a use of squad mechanics (or co-op) that looks genuinely meaningful and, more importantly, fun. Also from Ubi, ForHonor and sequel to the delightful Grow Home, GrowUp (yay!).

Across the rest of E3 thus far, EA's conference seemed to falter as a presentation, but I'm hopeful for Mass Effect, and the gameplay of Battlefield 1 looks visceral, if oddly fast-paced for a WW1 title. Bethesda's presentation brought a strong showcase to the table, while Microsoft's was strange. Two different consoles (not seeing the point in the Xbox One S if Scorpio is next year) and a focus on all their exclusives hitting PC as well as Xbox (PCMasterRace :P).

That leaves Sony, with a genuinely excellent and exciting conference along with a solid lineup of titles, with the live orchestra being a great starting point, bringing greater immersion to the entire show. Other than what's been touched upon above, they also showed more of the impressive HorizonZeroDawn, finally gave TheLastGuardian a release date(!), and, in general, made me much more open to the idea of picking up a PS4. Of particular note were the titles that surprised by subverting people's expectations. InfiniteWarfare returned after a, frankly, limp reveal, more than making up for it with an impressive gameplay demo that will likely change many opinions by showcasing a wonderful similarity to BattlestarGalactica, particularly in the space combat (those ships may as well be called Vipers), and fun-looking grapple hook (see also, Titanfall 2, for all your grappling needs) and zero-g mechanics. Similarly, the new Resident Evil looked downright chilling, like a reborn "PT"/Silent Hills until that Resident E(VII)L Biohazard logo appeared.

With the showfloor opening in a few hours and only a few reveals likely left, E3 (from an enjoyment standpoint) was pulled back from the fire by a packed PlaystationE3 conference. All that's left now is for @Nintendo's (90 minute!!) Legend Of Zelda live demo to live up to expectations tonight for us to be happy (well me and Ben for that one really).

That's it for Pandara's Box first, and likely lengthiest, blog. Next week, we will talk about what exactly we're working on (Hint: we're calling it #ProjectLeitmotif for now).

TL;DR: We're funded to make games, E3's exciting and ending soon.
