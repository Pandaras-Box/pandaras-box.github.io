Iceberg(s) Ahoy!! Digital and real barriers to work and play - #GGJ17

With our sudden return (in the eyes of you readers) with our last blog, I've been mulling over how to kick things off again, and, after recovering from the 48hr madness of 2017's Global Game Jam, it occured to me that the main antagonistic element in GGJ17 Belfast's very own "R.H.Itanic" had a neat metaphorical parallel with the trajectory of the team sometime after our last 2016 update!

So what nonsense is this now you think? Belfast didn't actually follow through so hard on its baffling affinity for the famed boating disaster that it had strapped itself to a raft and took the team into dangerous, ice-filled waters surely? Well of course not, but as I fired cannons into the things in (criminally overlooked - in favour of bug-fest Unity - AC:Black Flag) follow-up Rogue after the jam saw my team use them as obstacles, icebergs suddenly became the perfect way to explain where we'd vanished... For you see, the good (Box?) ship Pandara sailed true as best it could, but unfortunately the dreaded Iceberg of Nofunds loomed ever closer, we plotted a new course, all was well, before a Piratical Armada of personal circumstances emerged from the fog and crippled our honest vessel. We remained at port until the winter began to subside and repairs were completed as best possible, so here we are. I felt it might be nice to mark our return with the website launch, and with GGJ17 so near the timing just worked out to combine things with a locally relevant politically satirical return (more on that in a sec). Going forward, notable project updates will likely be smaller and less frequent as the team returns to juggling other means of financing themselves, we are very much in the planning stages of a new "normal" for Pandara's so expect an update on this in the future.

Right, onto the fun stuff then, namely my escapade over the weekend down to @farsetlabs - thanks again to those who organised locally, I'll let Glenn, one of said organisers, do the shout-outs here:

- for @GlobalGameJam. The theme was "Waves" and I was working with two friends that had been on my uni course, Cathal and Conor, both attempting a game jam for their first time, having talked about it numerous times in the past. Considering the lack of experience we held as a team dealing with the unique nature of a lengthy game jam (and some prior commitments that took them away for stretches during the weekend!) I think our humourous, political satire with it's wonderfully evocative reward for surviving 100 seconds can happily go down as a success. Credit goes to Cathal for the truly poetic blurb found with the web build over at its current home here: R.H.Itanic!
A fairly simple wave (heh) survival game where clicking creates shockwaves to knock away oncoming icebergs, said icy evils will both damage the ship on collision and speed up the chasing wave if they get past before being destroyed. Apart from our own work (to people not from NI, it's based on local humour/events) there was a wonderful variety on-show some of which can be seen at the Farset hub site with the personal standout for me being a game concerning screaming in co-op to escape a supermarket... yes really. Have a go at some of the bizarre but brilliant brain-children of Belfast if you have the time! :)
