Let's talk #ProjectLeitmotif a bit (& some @Qub_QCon, video game movies)


So we've After clearing up last week how exactly we've risen from the ashes (of university) with #Tranzfuser, I think it's fair that we fulfill our plan from last week, that of of discussing, at least a little bit, the title we're working on for the programme. Its working title, as seen in the title above, does give away one key aspect if you know the word ‘leitmotif’ means. If not, I'll let the Oxford Dictionary handle this one: Leitmotif - A recurrent theme throughout a musical or literary composition, associated with a particular person, idea, or situation. For our project, it is the musical application we're focused on, with audible leitmotifs being a key aspect of the battle system we are currently prototyping.

To compare our prototype to things you may already know, Project Leitmotif could be seen as what would happen if you took the battle system from Paper Mario, tossed in the musical gameplay of Guitar Hero, and then blinded that concoction with the same radioactive waste that the young Daredevil and the Baby Normal Untrained Turtles were so fond of. PL, then, is a musically inclined turn-based combat system where listening will take point amongst your senses as you play. The core system of play, that of an active turn-based combat system, contains some of the interactions added to the likes of the aforementioned Paper Mario, that of button presses when presented with a visual cue, but with a fundamental twist. Instead of reacting to visual cues, depending on the situation (attacking, defending, supporting etc) players will be reacting to audio cues - which is where those leitmotifs come in - in a variety of fun ways. These interactions are what we will be tweaking and experimenting with for the next few weeks, having spent the past couple laying the necessary groundwork for that to happen. We're very excited to be so close to showing off the system in the near future.
